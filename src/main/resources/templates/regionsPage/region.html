About Naryn

Naryn Province is the largest province in Kyrgyzstan, occupying the central part of the country. And, paradoxically, it is the most sparsely populated. It is a land of preserved nomadic Kyrgyz life, endless expanses and high mountains.

Almost all of Naryn Province, located in the southeastern part of Kyrgyzstan, is occupied by mountains. Periodically, mountain ranges alternate with desert steppes or large forests, intermountain valleys and lakes.

History

Historically, the area has always been inhabited by nomadic Kyrgyz who migrated to these places in the XI-XIII centuries and who still make up 99% of the population. In Naryn, like nowhere else, the nomadic way of life and life has been preserved unchanged for many centuries because the region has always been relatively distant from wars, trade routes, etc. During Soviet times, most of the Naryn region was closed to tourism, in order to preserve intact and preserve the amazing nature of the region. Thanks to this, now the Naryn region is an ideal place for ecotourism and wilderness trips in the bosom of nature. Like many other Central Asian cities, Naryn appeared as a small fortification on the trade routes that led from East Turkestan to Central Asia, to protect them from bandits. Until the middle of the 19th century there was no city as such in this area. With the accession of Central Asia to the Russian Empire in 1868 the Russian garrison was stationed here (Naryn fortifications), later appeared Naryn slobodka, and from that moment the construction of the city began. Through Naryn fortification with Semirechye Permanent postal service Russian Consulate in Kashgaria. After the October Revolution in 1917 Naryn came under the control of the Red Army, but in 1920 there was a clash between Soviet troops and the White Guards. After bloody battles, the city was repulsed and Soviet power was established here. In 1927, Naryn became the administrative center of the Naryn Province created at that time.

Climate

Naryn is the harshest city in the republic: winters here are long and very cold. The climate is sharply continental and arid, the average January temperature ranges from -18 to -20°С, sometimes it can drop to -38°С. Summers are warm, but very short. Precipitation is low, so the streets are usually dry and dusty. The average temperature in July is +20°C.

Tourism

Naryn attracts not only foreign tourists, but also Kyrgyz citizens who are passionate about domestic tourism. Nearby are picturesque passes and gorges, mountain rivers and lakes, waterfalls, eternal snows and glaciers. Also amazing is the high mountain landscape surrounding the city. There are many tourist routes from Naryn to the Tien Shan ranges.

* According to legend, the name of the city, Naryn, means broth of horse meat. But some sources argue that the word "naryn" comes from the Mongolian language and is translated into Russian as "sunny," or from Chinese as "narrow.

Sightseeings

Son-Kol

Sonköl is a large alpine lake, wedged in a basin between the inner spurs of the Tien Shan. It is located between the ridges of Sonkyol-Too and Moldo-Too, at an altitude of 3016 m above sea level in the northwestern part of Naryn region. It is a potential object of ecological tourism. The lake is surrounded by virgin pastures and a protected area. The nearest large settlement is the village of Chaek. Sonkyol comes from the Kyrgyz words "sono" (wild duck), "kol" (lake). That is, "the lake in which wild ducks live.
