* According to legend, the name of the city, Naryn, means broth of horse meat. But some sources argue that the word "naryn" comes from the Mongolian language and is translated into Russian as "sunny," or from Chinese as "narrow.

Sightseeings

Son-Kol

Sonköl is a large alpine lake, wedged in a basin between the inner spurs of the Tien Shan. It is located between the ridges of Sonkyol-Too and Moldo-Too, at an altitude of 3016 m above sea level in the northwestern part of Naryn region. It is a potential object of ecological tourism. The lake is surrounded by virgin pastures and a protected area. The nearest large settlement is the village of Chaek. Sonkyol comes from the Kyrgyz words "sono" (wild duck), "kol" (lake). That is, "the lake in which wild ducks live.
