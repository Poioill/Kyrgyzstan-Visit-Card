About Kyrgyzstan

Geography

Kyrgyzstan is located in Central Asia. in the north it borders on Kazakhstan, in the east - with China, in the west - with Uzbekistan, and in the southwest - with Tajikistan. access to the sea No. the total area of ​​this country is 198,500 sq. km., and the total the length of the state border is 3,878 km. more than 80% of the territory Kyrgyzstan is located in the Tien Shan mountain system. on in the southwest of the country there is the Pamir-Alai mountain system, and in the north and south-west - the fertile Ferghana and Chui valleys. the most the highest point is the peak of victory, whose height reaches 7,439 meters

History

The history of Kyrgyzstan covers periods from ancient petroglyphs to the Great Silk Road, from the Soviet Union to the adoption country of independence. kyrgyzstan has long been a historically an important point in the center of Asia, because it was at the confluence trade routes and empires. located right between the Chinese, Persian, Arabic, Indian, Turkic and Russian empires, the land that today forms kyrgyzstan, changed the history of many peoples, religions, cultures and travelers

Population

The permanent population of Kyrgyzstan is 6,389,500 people (as of January 1 2019). Until the 1960s, the population of the republic grew rapidly over account of migration and natural increase; the last one was especially significant among rural Kyrgyz, Uzbeks and others Central Asian peoples. November 25, 2015 the population of Kyrgyzstan reached 6 million people. most of the population is concentrated in the south of the republic - Osh, Jalal-Abad, Batken regions and the city of osh (3.4 million inhabitants or 53% of the population republics). also a significant proportion of the population concentrated in the Chui valley (Chui region and the city Bishkek; 1.9 million inhabitants or 31% of the population of the republic). the most densely populated regions - Osh and Chui.
